About

I am currently a PostDoc at the Institute for Automation and Applied Informatics at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology and interim research group leader within HelmholtzAI.
My research focuses on applying machine learning to problems related to future energy systems with a high share of renewable energy sources. I am especially interested in probabilistic forecasting and pattern recognition for energy time series, as well as reinforcement learning with uncertain inputs.

During my PhD, I was part of the research training group "Energystatusdata", funded by the German Research Foundation (DFG) and spent some months at the Mathematical Institute of the University of Oxford. I wrote my thesis on "Data-driven Methods for Demand-Side Flexibility in Energy Systems".

I received my Bachelor's degree in Economics (with a focus on econometrics) and Master's degree in Information System and Network Economics from the University of Freiburg (Germany) . I also spent a semester at the computer science and economics faculties of the University of Oslo.
